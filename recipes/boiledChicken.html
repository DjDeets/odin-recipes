Boiled Chicken

Description

Boiled chicken, the simplest way I know to make good chicken. It's very easy to clean up, as everything is contained in one giant pot.

This isn't meant to be a complete recipe, but rather a way to process a bunch of chicken into a source of protein for use in other recipes. It consists of 2 mandatory ingredients.

Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Water
  • Optional: Bay leaves

Steps

  1. Add water and salt to taste to a pot, all sized according to the amount of chicken to be prepared. This step comes first because you won't be able to safely taste the water until it's boiling after putting in the chicken. Don't stress, you can add more water and salt later after the water is safe to taste.
  2. Mix water and taste, the water should be salty. This is also a good time to add the Bay leaves (feel free to expriment).
  3. Carefully add chicken to the seasoned water. And immediately discard packaging, clean all surfaces which the raw chicken comes in contact with.
  4. Bring the water and chicken to a simmer. Lids are useful for this purpose.
  5. Simmer the heck out of the chicken until it looks fully cooked. Simmer longer if you plan on pulling the chicken into shreds.
  6. Carefully remove the chicken from the broth.
  7. Store the chicken broth for use. Can be drank as is, or used in soup or in place of water.
  8. Pre-process the chicken as you would like. Typically this means to either cut it or shred it.
  9. Store the chicken for later use.

You can now use this chicken to easily add protein to meals. Add it to your, salad, your pasta, serve it on rice, etc. You can brown it on a skillet, add seasonings (fancy), or just add bbq sauce to it and enjoy a pulled chicken sandwich. You also have protein infused broth which you can use to make stews, soups, pan sauces, and rice! Get creative!
